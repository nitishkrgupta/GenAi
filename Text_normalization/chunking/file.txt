Artificial Intelligence (AI) has transformed the way businesses and industries operate by automating repetitive tasks, analyzing large volumes of data, and supporting informed decision-making. Building on this foundation, Deep Learning (DL) uses neural networks with multiple layers to solve complex problems and has achieved remarkable success in areas such as computer vision, natural language processing (NLP), and speech recognition.

One of the most significant advancements in AI is Generative AI, which is capable of creating human-like text, images, code, audio, and other forms of content. This technology is powered by Large Language Models (LLMs) such as GPT, Llama, and Gemini, which are trained on massive datasets.  Their versatility has made them valuable tools across education, healthcare, finance, software development, and many other domains.

Despite their impressive capabilities, LLMs are not without limitations. They may occasionally produce incorrect, misleading, or outdated information, a problem commonly referred to as hallucination. This approach enhances the accuracy, reliability, and contextual relevance of AI-generated outputs by grounding responses in verified source documents.

The effectiveness of a RAG system depends heavily on proper document preprocessing techniques such as text cleaning, document chunking, embedding generation, and vector storage. As AI continues to evolve, technologies such as RAG, vector databases, and multimodal AI models are becoming fundamental components of modern intelligent applications, enabling more accurate, efficient, and context-aware AI systems.
